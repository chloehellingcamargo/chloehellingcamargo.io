AI · Agentic Workflows · Cross-team Impact

Automating the end-to-end PM workflow with AI — faster discovery, smarter prioritisation, with human strategy at every decision gate

Discovery was manual and slow — monitoring competitors, reading industry reports, synthesising user signals, all done by hand. I designed a multi-agent AI workflow where specialized agents continuously gather signal from call transcripts, app reviews, competitor moves, and market news, surfacing prioritised recommendations each week. AI handles the volume and synthesis. I review its output, apply the strategic context AI doesn't have, and nothing advances without my sign-off. Once I approve a direction, AI drafts specs and briefs based on discovery calls and my input, which I refine and approve before execution. The result is a faster, more informed product process where human judgement stays central to every decision. After validating it on my own team I rolled it out to other PMs across the org.

Freight Tech · Platform Strategy · WiseTech Global

Building the competitive moat: polygon geofencing, On-Duty toggle, multi-TMS connection, and low-signal queuing

Rather than chasing feature parity, I focused the roadmap on building durable competitive advantages that competitors couldn't easily replicate. Polygon geofencing (accuracy-first location tracking), multi-TMS connectivity (one driver, many carriers), and offline-first low-signal queuing became the product's core differentiation — and the basis for the platform's enterprise positioning and driver retention story.

Industry first — On-Duty toggle The single biggest concern drivers have with any mobile app is being tracked when they're off the clock. No other app on the market addressed this — until On-Duty. The feature gives drivers full control over when they share their location with dispatch, while ensuring dispatch always has the transparency they need: the toggle must be on to action a load. Off duty, drivers aren't tracked. On duty, dispatch has visibility. It's a simple idea with a profound impact — reducing the friction and mistrust that has historically defined the driver-dispatch relationship, and making driver retention a real commercial conversation with enterprise customers.
